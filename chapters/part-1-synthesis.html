The Pahlavi Crosses: Distribution and Dating

Stage 1 — Indian Ocean Rim (6th–7th century)
Cross location Region Date Status
Mylapore (Tamil Nadu)Coromandel6th centuryOriginal — oldest known
Anuradhapura (Sri Lanka)Indian Ocean island6th centuryContemporary with Mylapore
Agassaim/GoaKonkan7th centuryIndependent — pre-Islamisation

These three locations match precisely the sites Cosmas Indicopleustes describes (c. 550): Male (pepper coast), Kalyan (Konkan, where the bishop sits), Taprobane (Sri Lanka). The Persian Christian mercantile network was pan-coastal. No cross from Malabar proper in this period.

All Kerala crosses carry the same Pahlavi inscription as the Mylapore cross (or attempt to copy it). The churches where these crosses are found were built after the eighth/ninth centuries (with the possible exception of Muttuchira). The direction of transmission is Indian Ocean rim → Malabar interior: the Mylapore original was replicated and sent to the churches of the Malabar coast during the period of the Cēra Perumal consolidation.
