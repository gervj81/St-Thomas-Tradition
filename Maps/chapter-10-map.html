The Iberian Network
FIG. 10 · Administrative Architecture

Two crowns, two Sees, one global net.

After 1580 a single Habsburg ruled both Iberias. Two papal grants — the Spanish Patronato and the Portuguese Padroado — handed the Crown almost total ecclesiastical authority over the new world. Rome became a titular head, operationally bypassed.

From Madrid/Lisbon the network branched in two: westward across the Atlantic to Havana, Mexico, Lima and on to Manila by the Pacific galleon; eastward down Africa, around the Cape, up to Goa, Malacca, Macau and Nagasaki.

"The Spiritual Sword in Rome; the Temporal Sword at the dock."

A single dotted line on the Atlantic — the Line of Tordesillas (1494) — split the unknown world between them.
