## Key findings on the 52/72 chronology (from K. S. Mathew, ed., St. Thomas and India: Recent Research — verify page nos. when drafting)

- Earliest external witnesses (Acts of Thomas, Didascalia, patristics): India, apostolate, martyrdom, Mylapore/Calamina, relics to Edessa — NO 52/72 date-pair.
- 1578: Fr. Francis Dionisio SJ attests local traditions "written in books and sung in their songs" — existence of premodern local written/sung memory, not proof of the dates.
- AD 52 claim: Palayur Nagaragranthavari ("Kali year 3153 = AD 52, a foreign Sanyasi called Thomas came to our village"), reported via Bernard Thoma p. 99 — age/provenance of document not established.
- Song layer: existing palm-leaf texts from early 18th c. (three with 300+ years antiquity); P. U. Lukas printed 1910 (Ancient Songs of the Syrian Christians of Malabar, first printed folk-song collection in Kerala). Songs give AD 52 arrival + Mylapore martyrdom; one strand: arrival 52, Mylapore ~67, martyred at Chinnamala by Brahmin's spear (NOT 72); liturgical memory: died 21 Dhanu, bones 3 Karkadakam.
- AD 72 (3 July): earliest explicit = Ramban Pattu (claims composition 1601; ~9 MSS reported 1930; Koonammakkal worked from a 1987 photocopy). BUT Ramban Pattu's arrival is AD 50 at Malyamkara (return AD 51, Mylapore AD 59) — not the standard 52.
- Conclusion: the apostolic tradition is older than the chronological formula; the 52-72 pair is a later harmonized print-age form.

## Songs = multiple sacred geographies (not one itinerary)

- Margamkalippattu strand: begins in the Chola world (king wants palace like Solomon's; Havan sent west for a carpenter; Thomas as divine architect; Kali-temple refusal; Chinnamala martyrdom).
- Ramban Pattu strand: Malyamkara first, but Mylapore almost at once, back-and-forth itinerary.
- Later formula (Kodungallur 52 -> seven churches -> Mylapore 72) = a NEGOTIATED MAP fusing a Malabar foundation geography, a Chola/Mylapore martyrdom geography, and a seven-church geography.

## The Rosian counter-map

- Ros favoured east-to-west migration: Christians left Mylapore for Malabar (3rd-4th c., per Mundadan's dating of Ros's "1300 years before") under war/famine/persecution; Mylapore's apostolic see imagined as transferred to Cranganore, continued at Angamaly. Institutional motive: antiquity and rights of the see of Angamaly. (Mundadan; cross-check ch. 11 "The Rozian Puzzle".)
- Material Coromandel pole: Persian-style Mylapore cross; Portuguese identification of St Thomas Mount; "Santhome"/"St Thomas Mount" absent from the songs but burial always Mylapore.
- Manuscript apparatus: Land's Anecdota Syriaca (Leyden 1862; Malabar sections tr. Hosten); Taylor's 1837 translation of a Tamil legendary account (Keraladesam + Mylapore), republished Hosten 1923. (Tisserant-Hambye bibliography.)

## Perczel's role

- Four Apologetic Church Histories from India (The Harp XXIV, 2009; PDF in St thomas folder): apologetic history-writing predates print — 17th-18th c. Syriac/Garshuni histories (incl. the Mar Gabriel-circle history 1705-31, cf. Fenwick's citation on the sorcery legend). The 20th-c. print wars had manuscript-age ancestors; the community always wrote its past polemically; print + litigation raised the stakes.
- Perczel's manuscript turn (Syriac/Garshuni Malayalam digitization) = evidence of indigenous textual culture BEFORE modern print; use to avoid the crude "invention of tradition" thesis.

## Three-layer analytic (adopted)

1. Memory layer: songs, parish traditions, family claims (Pakalomattom, Knai Thoma), sacred geography, copper-plate memory.
2. Manuscript/learned layer: Syriac liturgical MSS, Garshuni Malayalam, padiyolas, Varthamanapusthakam (MS before print), pre-modern apologetic histories (Perczel).
3. Printed apologetic layer: Mackenzie, E. M. Philip (1908, out of the Seminary case), Bernard of St Thomas (Marthoma Christianikal), Medlycott (1905), Hosten, T. K. Joseph, K. M. Daniel, P. U. Lukas (1910), later Brown, Mundadan, Perczel.
